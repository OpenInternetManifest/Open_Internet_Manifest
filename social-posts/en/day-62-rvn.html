RVN: Assassination Attempt on President Trump – When Hatred and Hypocrisy Take Over Reality 🌀

Today another shooting incident involving President Trump. Shots fired at an event, Secret Service intervened, a suspect has been detained. Trump himself unharmed. Details still coming in.

Narrative (one side):
“This is obviously staged again for the midterms, to distract from Iran, oil prices, and everything that isn’t going according to plan. False flag!”

Narrative (other side):
“Deep State is trying to eliminate him for good.”

Reality – one layer deeper:
At this moment we still know too little for definitive conclusions. The investigation is ongoing. That is fact number one.

But what became immediately visible is shocking:
People openly posting “too bad it failed again”, “better luck next time”, or laughing at a possible assassination attempt on the President of the United States. 😔

This goes far beyond political disagreement. This is dehumanization.
When hatred becomes so normal that someone celebrates the death of another human being (even the President), we have entered a dangerous deeper layer of the manipulation of reality.

This is exactly what the 13-part series showed us:

  • Stanford Prison & role behavior → People in the “right bubble” feel morally justified to wish the worst.
  • Asch Conformity + False Consensus → The group cheers along, so it feels normal.
  • Dehumanization through group pressure → “He is no longer a human, he is the enemy.”
  • Stockholm + trauma bonding → People become emotionally addicted to their own narrative, even when it normalizes hatred and violence.
  • Foot-in-the-Door → It started with “he is dangerous”, it ends with “too bad the bullet missed”.

For years the same circles called critical questions “conspiracy thinking” and “a threat to democracy”. Now that the narrative no longer suits them, they embrace the wildest conspiracy theories themselves — or worse: open joy about violence against the President.

The core lesson
Manipulation doesn’t only work through authority or media. It works through tribal feeling that disables our moral compass. Both left and right are vulnerable to it, but the speed at which hatred becomes mainstream is alarming.

OIM way out
This is why we don’t play along in the narrative ping-pong.

  • Always demand primary sources and patience before embracing a story.
  • Recognize the mechanisms in yourself: do you wish harm on another human because he is “the enemy”?
  • Build parallel: communities where disagreement is allowed without dehumanization. Where sovereignty and responsibility stand above tribalism.
  • Refuse to feed the hate machine — even when “it’s the other side”.

The mirror is harsh today.
Not only for those who are cheering.
But for all of us who refuse to fall into the same trap.

Question to you
Have you seen in recent years how quickly “conspiracy theorist” turned into “too bad it missed”?
Which mechanism from the series do you see most clearly here?

And more importantly: how do we prevent hatred from becoming the new normal?

We keep building. Facts. Self-reflection. Parallel structures.
