Day 36 – RVN: Epstein → Demmink → Baybaşin – the pattern of silence 🕸️🔇
Why some cases are never truly solved.

Jeffrey Epstein was no isolated sex offender. He was a node in a network of power, blackmail, and silence. Thousands of hours of video, photos, logs – all meant to bind, break, and silence people. The real story often lies in what is NOT in the files.

In the Netherlands we have a case no one wants to touch: Joris Demmink and Hüseyin Baybaşin.

What is verifiable

  • Demmink was Secretary-General of Justice from 1998 to 2012 – highest civil servant in the ministry.
  • In 2013–2014 two Turkish men filed complaints against him for rape and sexual assault of minors in Turkey in the 1990s.
  • The Public Prosecution Service started a preliminary investigation (2014–2016) → dropped in 2016: “insufficient legal and convincing evidence”.
  • An authentic audio tape (2013) from a Turkish prosecutor mentioning Demmink as suspect was verified by multiple media (RTL, NRC).
  • Turkey summoned Demmink as a suspect in 2014 – the Netherlands did not extradite him.

Hüseyin Baybaşin has been serving life in the Netherlands since 1998 (convicted of murder and drug trafficking).

  • Baybaşin himself filed a complaint against Demmink in 2007 for pedophilia.
  • His lawyer Adèle van der Plas claims for years that Turkey blackmailed the Netherlands with Demmink’s alleged abuse to secure Baybaşin’s conviction with fabricated evidence (doctored wiretaps).
  • This claim appears in court documents and was discussed in media (EenVandaag, AD), but has never been proven criminally.

The pattern

  • Vulnerable minors from disadvantaged groups
  • Investigations that stop when they reach too high
  • Foreign pressure (Turkey in this case) on the Netherlands
  • A convicted man imprisoned for decades while accusations against the high official are dropped

Just like Epstein and Dutroux: child abuse as an instrument of blackmail and control – and a system that protects itself.

The facts rain is falling now, but the question remains: who filters it? Who decides which pieces get attention and which stay buried?

Tomorrow we continue: which thread do we pull next? Ronald Bernard? Or something new?

Read yourself. Check yourself. Build yourself.
What silence stands out to you? 🕸️🌱
