# Thesis 10

The digital cage is expanding into the real world ‚Äî and we are helping to carry it ourselves.

What we have accepted online for years ‚Äî mandatory accounts, permanent [**surveillance**](/EN/concepts/surveillance), algorithms deciding what you may see or do ‚Äî is slowly becoming normal in everyday life: digital passes for travel, apps that grant access to work or healthcare, systems from one company running government services and managing your data.

We are getting used to a single [**closed-source AI**](/EN/concepts/closed-source-ai) becoming the gatekeeper of knowledge, news, and human interaction ‚Äî not because it is inevitable, but because we confuse [**convenience**](/EN/concepts/convenience) with freedom.

If we do not break this normalization now, the [**walled garden**](/EN/concepts/walled-garden) will soon become the entire society.

Only a truly open internet can stop this expansion ‚Äî by showing that a free digital world is still possible, and thereby giving the physical world breathing room again.
