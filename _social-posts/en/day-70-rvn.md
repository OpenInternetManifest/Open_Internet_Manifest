**RVN: Illusory Progress 2.0 – What is it really?** 🌀

**Narrative:**  
“Technology, globalization and modern regulations are making our lives better, easier and more sustainable. We are progressing!”

**Reality:**

Illusory Progress is the phenomenon where something **appears** to improve, while in reality it often leads to more dependency, higher costs, less freedom and more control.

This is the start of a new 10-part series **Illusory Progress 2.0**. In the coming days we will cover topics such as:
- The car lie (lease vs ownership)
- Heat pumps and energy labels
- 15-minute cities and digital control
- Purchasing power and hidden inflation
- Feminism as illusory progress
- And ultimately the trap of democracy

**Examples of Illusory Progress:**
- We “get” an electric car → but have to lease it because we can no longer afford to buy one, and it is full of trackers.
- We “get” a heat pump → but pay three times as much for energy and become dependent on a well-functioning electricity grid.
- We “get” a 15-minute city → but lose the freedom to go and stand where we want.

**OIM way out**

Real progress is not more gadgets, rules or subsidies.  
Real progress is **more sovereignty, resilience and independence**.

That is why we build parallel structures:
- Our own food, energy and networks
- Verifiable information (hashes)
- Systems that are not dependent on the next “improvement” that actually ties us down further.
