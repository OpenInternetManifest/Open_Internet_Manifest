### RVN: “The Information War surrounding Q and QAnon” 🌀

**Narrative:**  
Q was a dangerous conspiracy movement (QAnon) full of violent extremists who believed in a satanic elite abusing children. It was a threat to democracy.

**Reality:**

What started as anonymous cryptic posts on 4chan grew into a worldwide phenomenon. But it was quickly hijacked and distorted.

Mainstream media and Big Tech created a new label: **QAnon**.  
This label was used as a broad brush to paint anyone asking questions about:
- Child trafficking and elite abuse
- Corruption in the deep state
- Media manipulation

…as dangerous lunatics.

**The real information war**

- Platforms (YouTube, Facebook, Twitter) began mass-censoring Q-related content.
- Media consistently called it “QAnon” and linked it to violence, while most “Anons” were peacefully doing research.
- The term “QAnon” became an all-purpose smear: anything that didn’t fit the official narrative was thrown into it.

This was not an organic reaction. This was a coordinated effort to demonize and isolate a decentralized movement that was critically examining power.

**The irony**

While QAnon was portrayed as dangerous, many of the underlying claims (Epstein, elite networks, censorship, corruption) were later confirmed. But the label stuck.

**OIM Way Out**

This is exactly why we need verifiable, decentralized systems.  
When central institutions (media, tech, government) work together to enforce a narrative, independent thinking and research become essential.

Q (and the Anons) were an early example of what we are now trying to build further: people who think for themselves, verify for themselves, and refuse to blindly trust institutions.

**Question to you**

Was Q really a dangerous movement, or was a movement asking uncomfortable questions systematically criminalized?
